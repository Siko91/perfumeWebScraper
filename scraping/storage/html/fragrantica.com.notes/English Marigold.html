English marigold is another name for Calendula officinalis, a plant native to southern Europe. This is a short-lived aromatic perennial plant distinguished by its yellow flowers that may appear all year long where conditions are suitable. Pot marigold florets are edible and they are typically used for salads and other dishes. The plant is also used as a medicinal herb and as a coloring agent for fabrics, food and cosmetics.
