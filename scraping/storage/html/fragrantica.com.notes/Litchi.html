LITCHI LYCHEE
 
 
 
Botanical Name – Litchi chinensis
 
 Family – Sapindaceae
 
 Common Name – Litchi, Lychee
 
Litchi is known as a very delicious fruit and it is my favorite too….really…..whenever the name lychee comes to mind…my mouth starts watering. The red-skinned fruit contains a single seed, surrounded by a juicy sweet aromatic aril or flesh.
  
 
 
Observation of traits at experimental garden at experimental station
 
 
 
Lychee or litchi (Litchi chinensis Sonn.) originated in the area between southern China, northern Vietnam and the Malay Peninsula, but is now cultivated in many countries with sub-tropical climates. It isa very well-known fruit in Asia, but relatively rare in Africa, the Middle East and America.
 
The family Sapindaceae represents the attractive, eye-catching fruit of the sub- family Nepheleae from the orient. Lychee (Litchi chinensis), longan (Euphoria longan), rambutan (Nephelium lappaceum) and pulasan (Nephelium mutabile) are similar trees, but distinctive in fruit morphology and ecology. Lychee is the most economically significant member of this group.
 
 
Lychee is popular as one of the kings of tropical fruit and has a very long history in Asia. The fruits are very attractive, with bright red skin covered by angular or conical protuberances. The fruit Longan resembles lychee, but the fruits are smaller, smoother, yellow-tan to brown, milder in flavor and less acidic. Rambutan and pulasan are also similar to lychee, with red or yellow skin, however, long hairs or spinterns replace the protuberances.
  
 
 

 Lychee is a long-lived, evergreen tree up to 10 to 15 m tall that produces its new leaves, flowers and fruit on terminal branches. In some cultivars, the branches are crooked or twisting and spreading, forming a crown broader than high, while in other cultivars, the branches are straight and upright, forming a compact, rounded crown.
 
The leaves are alternative and compound, with two to five leaflets. The new flushes become red-brown when immature and light to dark green as they mature. The inflorescences are in the form of many-branched panicles and the flowers are small, yellowish-white, functionally male or female and without petals. Functionally, male flowers have six to ten stamens.
 
 
Litchi flowers and fruits
 
 
 
The fruit shows variability in size, can be round, ovoid or heart-shaped, and from 2.0 to 3.5 cm in diameter. The skin can be smooth or rough with distinct protuberances, thick or thin, and pink-red, bright red, or purple-red. The flesh or aril is an outgrowth of the outer cells of the seed coat, and in good cultivars may comprise 80 percent of the fruit's weight. The aril is generally translucent white, juicy or firm, and sweet and aromatic in better cultivars. Many cultivars can be distinguished by their flavor and aroma.
 
The red color of litchi fruit is due to the activity and presence of different types and amounts of a pigment i.e. anthocyanin (cyanidine-3-glucoside, cyanidine-3-galactoside, pelargonaidine-3-glucoside). Browning of pericarp is due to the action of Polyphenol oxidase and peroxidase on anthocyanin, leading to damage or deterioration. The chemical process of browning/decolorization of pericarp can be stopped or decelerated by treatment just after harvesting.
 
 
Artificial Cross Pollination meant for production of hybrid varieties
 
 
 
Due to the reduced storage capacity of litchi, conservation of color and odor is very necessary for transporting to distant places or abroad. For this, the sulphitation process is a famous and scientific technique, in which sulphur mixed air is blown over litchi for 30-60 minutes in a closed chamber. This colorizes the pericarp as light yellow and thereafter the fruits are kept at 4°C temp. This technique helps to conserve litchi fruits and after reaching their final destination, the fruit regains its natural color.
 
The fruit contains a single dark brown seed. Seeds are bold but in some cultivars, seeds are partially developed, due to failure of pollination, referred to as "chicken-tongue" seed. The trees with small seeded fruits are prized because of the greater portion of pulp.
 
India is the second largest producer after China, with over 500,000 tons in a good year. However, most of the crop is sold locally, with little interest in exports until fairly recently. The major cultivars of Litchi in India are Shahi, China, Calcuttia, Bedana, Late Bedana and Longia.
 
 
Abundant fruiting in good crop season
 
 
 
The Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations has developed CODEX standards for exports of fresh lychee. Mature fruit should have a predominantly red skin, with only a small area of green allowed. The diameter of the fruit should be larger than 20 or 25 mm for second class or standard fruit, and larger than 33 mm for extra class fruit. The total soluble solids content should be greater than 18 percent. The residue for sulphur in the flesh should not exceed 10 mg per kg.
 
Lychee, being exacting in climatic and soil requirements, has limited distribution. It is highly specific to climatic requirement for its establishment, plant growth and fruiting, and consequently spread of area. A moist atmosphere, occasional rainfall, a cool dry winter free from frost and hot winds are ideal for its cultivation. It is cultivated in the states of Bihar, Tripura, West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Punjab and Haryana of India. Of the total production of lychee in India, 74 percent is contributed by Bihar. The second largest lychee producing state is West Bengal, followed by Tripura and Assam.
 
The National Research Centre for Litchi (Indian Council of Agriculture Research), Muzaffarpur (Bihar), India, engaged in the systematic collection, conservation and characterization of litchi cultivars and worked on various breeding and genetic engineering techniques to develop high-yielding cultivars. They have also developed some post-harvest management and technologies for better utilization of the fruit. The first author was a research associate and worked on various aspects of litchi during the period.
 
The fruit contains protein, fat, carbohydrate, Calcium, Iron, thiamin, riboflavin, niacin, and ascorbic acid.
 
 
 
 
 
In China, lychee can be used fresh, dried or processed. Fruit can be dried in the sun or in ovens, with good retention of flavor. Most of the dried fruits are sold locally, with some exported to other countries in the Region.
 
In India, fresh, peeled litchi fruits are eaten directly, and may be taken in the form of fresh aril juice. They are commonly added to fruit cups and fruit salads. The fruits may be layered with pistachio ice cream and whipped cream in parfait glasses, as dessert. Pureed lychees are added to ice cream mix.
 
 
Sherbet is made by extracting the juice from fresh, seeded lychees and adding it to a mixture of prepared plain gelatin, hot milk, light cream, sugar and a little lemon juice, and freezing. Peeled, seeded lychees are canned in sugar syrup in India and have been exported from China for many years.
 
 
 
 
 
Source: Photographs from experimental garden and farm by Aarti Kumari (Singh) from NRC on Litchi, Muzaffarpur, Bihar.
