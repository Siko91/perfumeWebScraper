 Linden blossom is one of those floral fragrances that I have been looking for in perfume from the very beginning of my hobby. I searched for it, but encountered it less often than I would like to admit. The authentic smell of the blooming linden tree is rarely found in perfumery, which is quite sad - the majority of truly beautiful perfumes with this note are no longer produced or were at some point reformulated. However, I would still like to share the results of my research on the topic. Some editions are still available. Others can be found at auctions. And then there are the linden blossom fragrances created by Russian artisan perfumers.
 
Linden blossom wilts quickly. But a fragrance with a linden blossom scent can revive in your mind the days when the trees were in full bloom, and do so any time you want…  The main thing here is to find a fragrance that fits your perception of blossoming linden trees. 
 
 
A French linden tree, the famous Tilleul by D'Orsay brings early June in Paris, blooming linden trees in the Luxembourg Gardens, warm transparent air, the spicy scent of cut wilted grass, and the fragrance of blossoming acacia… The honey-tinged linden blossom-acacia scent is immaculate in Tilleul. There is exactly as much honey as there is in the natural blossoms, and not a bit more. The intense yet fresh sweetness is offset by the subtle bitterness of absinthe and tobacco.
 
Out of all the linden blossom fragrances, Tilleul by D'Orsay is the most elegant, exactly because it is not a soliflore, and its honeylike qualities do not suppress the other notes.
 
 
Truth be told, I am talking about the "old" version, packaged in a yellow box. The new version (which comes in a green box) disappointed me: it has acquired ozone and aquatic notes, plus the scent of under ripe watermelon, while the linden blossom is not as apparent as it used to be in the old version. It's as if its fragrance was muffled by the piercing note of musk… Such a pity. It was a good perfume. It is possible that it was even greater before my version. But by now it is difficult to find the version of Tilleul by D'Orsay that I enjoy, let alone any older versions…
 
I haven't tried any earlier versions though. I don't know whether it's worth even dreaming about those ones.
 
D'Orsay offered two more "sequels" on the theme: both are quite good, but they are not especially meant for those looking for a true linden blossom scent.
 
Tilleul Friction de Nuit by D'Orsay brings a more distinct linden blossom scent than in the reformulated Tilleul by D'Orsay, but the main bouquet is green and herbal; the scents of cut grass, mint, as well as the unlisted cilantro, dill, and some harsh smelling, unidentifiable fresh flowers muffle the linden blossom, pushing it to the back.
 
Tilleul Pour La Nuit by D'Orsay smells like apothecary chamomile, hay and linden herbal tea. It does have a certain natural charm to it; this perfume can be a great scent for relaxing, but it is no longer the blooming linden tree…
 
 
Meanwhile, the linden blossom in French Lime Blossom by Jo Malone London is so exquisite and delicate! Another one that's no longer produced, but you can still find it.
  
 
 
It is the scent of summer, of cool bodies of water, and reed thickets. It is the fragrance of a blooming linden tree that is only starting to thicken into an intoxicating honey kind of sweetness. It is linden blossom washed by a rain shower; the fragrance of raindrops falling down from the linden tree branches smelling like flowers and greenery. On one of the tree's branches, hiding somewhere behind the leaves and blossoms there sits the eternally young Puck playing his pipe. His magical melody makes the fragrance magical, too... It is bitter, fresh and sweet.
 
 
 
Unter den Linden by April Aromatics: very honey like, very sweet, creating a sensation of a full immersion into the linden blossom scent – something similar is probably felt by insects, even though they actually have a very different kind of olfactory perception than we do… The linden tree in this fragrance is as immense as the World Tree, oozing the sweetest kind of nectar or even honey. The sweetness of linden blossom is augmented by the fragrance of magnolia and the fresh spiciness of citruses, which in turn helps to create the sensation of total immersion into the linden blossom fragrance as opposed to linden blossom honey.
 
No. 10 Linde Berlin by Frau Tonis Parfum is linden blossom, greenery, shade, coolness, and a light breeze. The fragrance gradually becomes warmer and sweeter, thickening into honey. It is a simple, easy-to-understand perfect kind of beauty.
 
Waltz No.14, a fragrance from Tokyo Milk Parfumerie Curiosite, is no longer listed on Margot Elena's official website, which is a pity. It was a wonderful, cheerful fragrance of linden blossom with notes of jasmine and lilacs, sweet and fresh. It was neither as sophisticated as Tilleul by D'Orsay  and French Lime Blossom by Jo Malone London, nor as bright and grandiose as Unter den Linden by April Aromatics, but it was charming and cheerful as a little girl in a white sundress and white sandals  dancing to a street musician playing the violin. It had her joie de vivre, her simple desire to dance, the summer, the linden blossoms' aroma, and the sounds of a waltz in the air…
 
 
In the Library of Flowers, a series of eaux de parfum and perfumed creams also produced Margot Elena's company, there is Linden: a nice fragrance that smells of clover, linden blossom, flower honey, and meadow herbs. However, it lacks the easy charm, the sophistication that could be felt in Waltz No.14.
 
L'Ete en Douce by L'Artisan Parfumeur: the linden trees are somewhere at a distance, the breeze brings over their fragrance, yet up close you have the scent of linden herbal tea, or maybe the linden tisane that Aunt Leonie dipped her madeleine biscuit in before giving it to Proust, making his childhood forever sweet. It is possible that the aroma of a madeleine biscuit is also there, yet it is faint, gradually dissolving in a sweetish kind of woody aroma and the bitter-sweet scent of chamomile tea and dandelions. A charming perfume, quiet and gentle. And again, this was me talking  about the older version...
 
The new version of L'Ete en Douce by L'Artisan Parfumeur has a harsher musk note and a stronger scent of hay. Nevertheless, the perfume is still very pleasant and the linden blossom fragrance in it is authentic.
 
Linden blossom is also felt in La Chasse aux Papillons by L'Artisan Parfumeur, but is combined with the scents of a gentle innocent tuberose, some cool jasmine, sunny orange blossom, and a whole meadow of redolent honey giving wild flowers... I have to say that in my opinion, the linden blossom in La Chasse aux Papillons has been collected, but not dried yet - it lies there on the windowsill on a sheet of waxed paper filling the entire house with its aroma… Or it is a wilting linden tree. A dry pollen like fragrance, but it still smells like linden blossom anyway.
 
 
La Chasse aux Papillons Extreme by L'Artisan Parfumeur is less delicate or harmonious, it is easier to understand and louder, with notes of linden blossom and linden blossom honey mixed with (ripe) apricot juice. Again we find the same white floral bouquet here as well (tuberose, jasmine and orange blossom).
 
Schöne Linden 05 by Krigler is a vivid fragrance whose variety of lush florals  reminds me of chintz fabric. Linden blossom is surrounded here by tuberose, gardenia, carnation, lilacs, and violets, with each flower featured at its sweetest stage; the tuberose and gardenia are downright syrupy, the lilacs and violets are sugared, and the carnation is spicy, hot, and sweet. The linden blossom here is presented simultaneously as the blossoms on the branches and the fragrance of linden blossom honey.
 
 
Russian brand Ladanika has some wonderful linden blossom fragrances. 
 
Dyhanie Leta ("Breath of Summer" by Yanina Yakusheva) is a fragrance where linden blossom performs the solo. The delicate, pollen like scent of linden blossom is supplemented by notes of mimosa, acacia, tropical ylang-ylang, and dark tropical vanilla. To me, it is the fragrance of the linden tree from my childhood, whose sweet redolence seemed like a miracle each summer and I felt like breathing it in as much as I could, keeping it for myself, inside...  This is pure bliss.
 
Tyomnye Allei ("Shadowed Paths" by Valeria Karmanova) is another version of the sweet fragrance of linden blossom; honeylike, enveloping, hot. The summer heat is at its fullest; the scents of clover and melilots, which are honey like here as well, fill the hot air, and there is also hay that smells sweet and spicy. The base holds a thick Oriental-chocolatey aroma, yet it does not distract from the main theme: a summery fragrance of linden blossom and blooming meadows. No shadowed paths, no visible signs of Ivan Bunin's literary universe as the name suggests, but more Sergey Esenin's expansive Russian fields in the summer. This fragrance is especially good in the winter; use it to reminisce about the summer.
 
 
And if someone wants to have some linden blossom honey, linden blossom tisane, or some strong tea with a bit of samovar smoke, seek out Russkaya skazka ("Russian Fairytale", by Nikolay Eryomin); it is a tea-honey-and-smoke fragrance executed with a lot of sophistication… Another one I would not recommend for the summer; it is perfect to warm yourself up with in winter, but it is too sweet and hot for the summer.
 
An unusual rendition of linden blossom's scent is the Honey Linden Blossom  from Odoratika by Valeria Karmanova. This is honey-sweet yet dry, with lots of pollen and powder; a gooey, somewhat bitter honey, and a just as thick amber aroma.
 
Tilleul Reflexion by Evgeny Firsanov is a walk under the linden trees on a happy summer day. A path between blossoming linden trees, and you're inhaling the sweet air in a state of utter bliss. A single flower in boom that you put so close to your face that it seems enormous, and you feel like you're drowning in its nectar, bathing in its pollen, inhaling the fragrance. There is linden blossom honey here, too; fresh, trickling down the honeycomb. Later, the fragrance takes you to the meadows, to their wild flowers and herbs. And then it thickens again, turning into nectar.
 
 
For those who might find all of the above mentioned fragrances by Russian perfumers overly sweet (despite the fact that a blossoming linden tree does smell sweet), there is a linden blossom fragrance that smells like linden blossom after the rain, or even during a rainshower… "Apres la Pluie" (After the Rain) by Esquisse Parfum from Marina Volkova is half linden blossom and half cool rain moisture; one senses the scent of wet grass, bergamot, and jasmine green tea here as well.
 
The most unusual linden blossom fragrance created by Russian artisan perfumers is Galina Kosterina's Clair de Tilleul from Tamanu Perfumes. All creations from this perfumer smell like natural plants in the wild, as if the air was steeped in herbs. She is most successful when working with wormwood; a difficult note that is often present in perfumery, yet normally reveals itself as the aroma of absinthe, not the natural wormwood pounded lightly between one's palms. Galina Kosterina creates a truly natural wormwood note that she seems to use in every perfume she makes.
 
 
In Clair de Tilleul she uses bitterness too, in addition to the expected sweetness. All of the sweetness comes from the linden blossom and you feel a blooming tree surrounded by bees busily buzzing around it, but also flowers that had already been gathered, and the a fragrant cloud of steam rising from a mug of hot linden blossom tisane… The bitterness comes from herbs; they have only just been cut, but not dried yet. It's yarrow, tansy, wormwood. All of it together gives a wonderful effect of naturalness and purity to the fragrance. I do understand however that the scent is not going to be a crowd pleaser; for many it will be too natural and "not perfumery-like".
 
There are other perfumes claiming they feature the linden blossom note among the fragrances I have tried… However, in my personal opinion, they do not possess the explicit fragrance of blooming linden trees. I don't want to say that it is not there, it is quite possible that you'll be luckier than me, so it would be wrong of me to exclude them from this review.
