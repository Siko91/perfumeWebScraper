I love citrus colognes, and try to find a new favorite at every event and show I attend. I did it at Fragranze too; this time my personal best citrus fragrance was Note de Yuzu, a collaborative work of two brands from Paris: the fashion house of Maison Kitsune and the perfume house of Heeley.
 
 
Note de Yuzu is the first perfume creation for Maison Kitsune, in which James Heeley intended to convey the cheerful energy of the brand, as well as its connection with both the Japanese and the French culture. Citrus colognes are quite traditional for the French, they draw summery images, share their liveliness and in general are a good start of the day.
 
Japanese people appreciate citruses no less, and yuzu in particular. Yuzu fruit ripens towards the end of December, and is associated with the Winter spirit for people in Japan.
 
On the day of the winter solstice (toji), the Japanese take a traditional bath with yuzu fruit in it. This ritual is called yuzuyu or yuzuburo. The fruits just float in the water like yellow duckies, exuding their special citrus aroma.
 
 
It is considered that yuzu fruit shares its bright energy, pleases the eye, softens the skin, improves the bloodstream, relaxes the muscles and make you look and feel younger.
 
The perfume Note de Yuzu Maison Kitsune X Heeley starts with a happy smile and goosebumps of pleasure. It is sweet and uplifting, with a cunning sparkle inside. There is mandarin, refreshing mint, grapefruit, plenty of cold and transparent water and a feeling of excitement for the approaching winter holidays.
 
The aldehidic sharpness and a resinous nuance, so common for yuzu, are hidden by sweet mandarin and orange fruit. A bit of powder and a nuance of bitter citrus peel are craftily incorporated in a very clean fragrance structure. This steamy, healthy nuance lasts and lasts for your pleasure, till the base notes enter into their rights.
 
The top notes remind me of the bright and positive Insense Givenchy and Oyedo Diptyque, but only in their mood, not the actual ingredients. Pure yuzu oil has a pronounced terpenic nuance and even a woody-coniferous smell with a dash of salt. They've kept this in Note de Yuzu, a strange mineral salty note preceding the white musks and vetiver, which is also quite salty.
 
 
Note de Yuzu Maison Kitsune X Heeley is the first personal fragrance for Maison Kitsune made by Heeley, but before that, 4 aromatic candles were launched in October of 2012: Mint, Moss, Blackberry and Blueberry. I hope that Note de Yuzu will not be the only fragrance they make together. There are so many appealing aromatic subjects to explore, like shiso, Japanese cedar sugi, hinoki, French salt Fleur de Sel and Japanese sea salt Moshio, etc.
 
Note de Yuzu Maison Kitsune X Heeley will be available in Maison Kitsune boutiques and Heeley stockists from September 28. The fragrance (in Eau de Parfum concentration) will be packaged in 50ml (90 euro) and 100ml (130 euro).
 

 By Sergey Borisov
