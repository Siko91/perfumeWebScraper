Licorice is the root of Glycyrrhiza glabra and it is commonly used as a medicine and for food production. Licorice is also known as "sweet root" because it contains a chemical compound named anethole and glycyrrhizin, which is about 50 times sweeter than sugar. The aroma of licorice is similar to those of anise, star anise, tarragon, sassafras, and fennel.
