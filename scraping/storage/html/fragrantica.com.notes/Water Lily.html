The Water Nymph by Herbert James Draper, 1908
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Nymphea is derived from Greek mythology where nymphs were minor deities associated with specific locations, including springs or small bodies of water. Genus Nymphea is representing their species as water lily. Water lily flowers have wonderful beauty and fragrance, lasting only a few days. Water lilies are the most picturesque of our native plants. According to Indian mystics, lotus is known as the better half of the sun while water lily is of the moon. Water lilies are characterized by their circular floating leaves and white or yellow flowers. They grow at the edges of slow flowing rivers, in canals, lakes and ponds. They can grow in water of up to 5 m deep, but prefer depths of between 1 m and 3 m.
 
 
 
 
 
 
FAMILY – Nympheaceae
 GENUS - Nymphea
 SPECIES - Genus Nymphea is represented by about 50 aquatic plant species, which has a cosmopolitan distribution. Some species of water lily and their common names are as follows:
 
 
 
Nymphaea alba
 
Common Name - European White Water lily and White Lotus
 
Nymphaea odorata
 
Common Name - Fragrant Water Lily and Beaver Root
 
Nymphaea nouchali Synonym N. stellata
 
Common Name - Red and blue water lily, Blue star water lily, Star lotus
 
Nymphaea caerulea
 
Common Name - Blue Egyptian water lily and sacred blue lily
 
Nymphaea capensis
 
Common Name - Cape blue water lily
 
Nymphaea leibergii
 
Common Name - Dwarf Water lily and Leiberg's water lily
 
Nymphaea lotus
 
Common Name - Tiger Lotus, White lotus and Egyptian White Water-lily
 
Nymphaea mexicana
 
Common Name - Yellow Water lily, Mexican Water lily and Banana Water lily
 
Nymphaea pubescens
 
Common Name - Hairy water lily or Pink water-lily
 
 
 
Nymphea odorata, N. nouchali Syn. N. Stellata and N. alba are more common and important species of water lily group.
 
 
 
 
 
N. odorata is known as fragrant white water lily, having fragrant, white or pink flowers
 with flat, round, floating leaves. It usually flowers only from early morning until noon. Each flower lasts only 3-4 days, and the flowers are quite fragrant. Each flower has 4 greenish white sepals on the bottom that rest on the water surface, and about 20-35 white petals above. It was utilized in many ways by Native Americans in the eastern United States. The leaves and flowers were used as cooling compresses. The rhizomes were also used as food and the young leaves and lower buds were eaten as a vegetable. The seeds were fried and eaten or ground into flour.
 
 
N. nuochali is a day blooming plant with floating, round and green leaves. It is a large perennial herb which is found growing throughout the India and southeastern Asia. This water lily has a beautiful flower which is the national flower of Bangladesh (where it is known as “Shapla”) and Sri Lanka (where it is known as “Nil Mahanel”). The red and blue water lily is used as a decorative and ornamental plant due to its spectacular flowers. Nymphea nuochali is consider a medicinal plant in India and being utilized in Ophthalmic, wounds, sores, diseases of the mouth, headache, skin disease, as a styptic, for alcoholic delirium, muscoskeletal disorders, gynecological problems and hair fall etc. The alcoholic extract of defatted fruits produces mild sedation and ataxia.
 

 Nymph by Emile Albert Gruppe
 
N. alba, known as the European Water lily, has white flowers. It is a deciduous perennial plant which flourishes in full sun. Its foliage is dark green glossy leaves can be up to 30 cm across and its flowers are semi-double pure white and cup shaped. It grows in water 30-150 cm deep and prefers still to slow-moving water in a large pond or lake. N. alba leaves yielded alpha-aminoadipic acid, luteolin-8-C-beta-D-glucopyranosoid and gossypitrin, while the flowers yield cardiac glucoside nymphalin. Nymphalin shows sedative action in small doses. A decoction of flowers works as a cardiac tonic in palpitation.
