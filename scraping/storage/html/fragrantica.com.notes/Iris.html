The second eponymous fragrance by niche haven Aedes de Venustas in Greenwich Village in New York City is named Iris Nazarena and created by acclaimed German perfumer Ralf Schwieger (with the perfume-producing company Mane), winner of the Fragrance Foundation France Prix des Experts, 2012, as well as creator of such successful fragrances as Eau des Merveilles (Hermès), Lipstick Rose (Frédéric Malle), The Afternoon of a Faun, and Philippine Houseboy aka Fils de Dieu (Etat Libre d' Orange) and Orange Sanguine (Atelier Cologne).
 
 
Ralf Schweiger
 
 
 
Aedes de Venustas is no newcomer to having fragrances specifically created for their discerning patronage: This perfume boutique with its French boudoir baroque flair was founded in 1995 by Karl Bradl and Robert Gerstner, and soon became a literal shrine where perfume enthusiasts and true connoisseurs honor the sacred worship of scent, thanks to the vast variety and meticulous attention of the owners to selecting products from all over the world. The fragrances which were created to reflect the aesthetics and concept of the line were introduced with an incensy-musky home fragrance co-created with L'Artisan Parfumeur and in 2012 with their collaboration with Bertrand Duchaufour the first eau de parfum Aedes de Venustas was a tangible reality.
 
 
This second fragrance, this time dressed in somber grey instead of the sumptuous purple of the first one, is mysterious, mystical and sensual, placing the flower of iris is at the center of the new creation; but this is not the pale or rooty iris that we know in other fragrances tagged with "iris" in their name or notes list, but a composition that focuses on the the Bismarck variety, also known by the name of Nazarene iris thanks to growing in the mountains east of Nazareth.
 
 
The mysterious beauty of the flower, with its delicate brown spots and purple, bluish purple veins, seduced Karl Bradl and from then on the search for the right formula became a quest for the brand.
 
With Iris Nazarena, Ralf Schwieger explains "I had to face two major challenges: to find a point of difference with Chanel No. 19, which has always been a reference point for iris-based scents and an unsurpassable model since its launch in 1971, and to incorporate transparency so that the beauty of the Nazarene iris would be best expressed".
 
Ambrette, with its facets of pear, rose and musk, is luminous on the top notes while juniper berries introduce the theme of incense. The fresh and vivid green star anise evokes the stylized leaves and stems of the flower with woody notes such as patchouli and vetiver alluding to the roots in the earth. The clay like earthiness is further warmed by touches of cloves and oud.
 
 
 
 
Top: iris, ambrette, juniper berries, star anise
 Heart: leather, oud, cloves, rose de Mai
 Base: incense, woods, musk, vetiver
 
 
 
 
 
Aedes de Venustas Iris Nazarene will be available as an Eau de Parfum in 100ml/ 3.4oz bottles from June 2013.
