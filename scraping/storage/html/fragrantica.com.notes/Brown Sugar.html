Brown sugar is produced either by adding molasses to refined white sugar crystals, or by the deliberate incomplete refining of white sugar. The molasses gives the sweet sugar a distinct color ranging from light brown to dark brown, as well as a rich flavor with caramel overtones and a distinct aroma. Brown sugar retains more moisture than white sugar and when used in baking will result in softer baked goods that retain their moisture well.
