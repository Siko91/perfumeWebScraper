Brazilian Rosewood that's used in perfumery is from the Aniba rosaedora tree (not to be confused with the Dalbergia genus) and has a floral, slightly rose-like odor. It takes a massive amount of the tree’s wood to extract a tiny bit of oil, which makes it no wonder that Brazilian rosewood trees are now protected under CITES.
